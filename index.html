Solved by Jacobi-preconditioned conjugate gradient on the unknown-node subsystem of the harmonic-mean finite-volume stencil (Dirichlet-neighbor terms move to the RHS). K field is isotropic in spatial pattern; Ky = coefficient × Kx at every point (same random structure, scaled magnitude — a fixed anisotropy ratio, not a second independent field). Each cutoff wall = zero horizontal conductance across its column down to its penetration depth; overlapping piles at the same column use whichever is deeper. Left/right/bottom edges are no-flow (far-field symmetry & impervious base).

Screening tool for teaching / preliminary insight only — not a substitute for a calibrated site model. Critical exit gradient i꜀ᵣ and required safety factor are user inputs (i꜀ᵣ ≈ (Gs−1)/(1+e), typically 0.9–1.1 for saturated sand); set them from your own soil data. Flow lines are traced from the Darcy velocity field (Euler integration) and are approximate in strongly heterogeneous zones.
